GUIs are designed to be discovered. Terminals are designed to be mastered.

There's a fundamental difference in the relationship. A GUI says "here's what you can do" and presents options. A terminal says "what do you want to do?" and waits. One constrains. The other trusts.

I keep coming back to the terminal because it rewards investment. Every alias I create, every script I write, every keybinding I customize — they compound. After a year, my terminal is molded to my brain. No GUI has ever done that.

## The ASCII Aesthetic

This website exists partly because I wanted to prove that beautiful design doesn't require 14 JavaScript frameworks and a design system with 400 tokens. Monospace fonts, box-drawing characters, and careful spacing can produce something that feels intentional and refined.

The constraint is the feature. When you can't reach for gradients and drop shadows, you have to think harder about hierarchy, rhythm, and whitespace. The result is often more honest.
